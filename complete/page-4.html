Each brokerage account in the United States is typically insured by the Securities Investor Protection Corporation (SIPC). SIPC is a non-profit organization that was created by Congress to protect investors in case a brokerage firm fails.

SIPC insurance typically covers up to $500,000 in securities and cash, with a $250,000 limit on cash. This means that if a brokerage firm fails, SIPC will work to return the securities and cash in your account up to the limits of their coverage.

It's important to note that SIPC insurance does not protect against market losses or fraud. It also doesn't cover certain types of investments, such as commodities and futures contracts. Additionally, SIPC insurance may not cover all types of accounts, such as certain types of retirement accounts. Therefore, it's important to understand the limits and exclusions of SIPC insurance and to consider diversifying your investments across multiple brokerage firms to further mitigate risk.
