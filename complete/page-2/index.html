Unrealized and Realized Gains are two types of investment returns. Unrealized gains refer to the increase in the value of an investment that has not yet been sold. This means that while the investment has increased in value on paper, you have not actually earned a profit until you sell the investment. On the other hand, realized gains refer to the actual profit you make when you sell an investment that has increased in value. It's important to keep in mind that realized gains are subject to taxes, while unrealized gains are not.

Dividends Received refer to the payments made by a company to its shareholders from its profits. If you own shares in a company that pays dividends, you will receive a portion of the company's earnings in the form of regular payments. These payments can be made in cash or additional shares of stock, and they represent a return on your investment in the company. Dividends received can be reinvested into the company or used for personal expenses. It's important to note that not all companies pay dividends, and the amount and frequency of dividend payments can vary depending on the company's financial performance and policies.

Funds Deposited and Funds Withdrawn refer to the movement of money in and out of a financial account. When you deposit funds into an account, you are adding money to it. For example, if you deposit $100 into a brokerage account, your funds deposited would be $100. On the other hand, when you withdraw funds from an account, you are taking money out of it. For example, if you transfer $100 from a brokerage account to your bank account, your funds withdrawn would be $100.
